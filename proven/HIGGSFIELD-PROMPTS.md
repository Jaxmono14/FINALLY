## How to Use These Prompts

Each prompt maps to a campaign concept and copy framework from the campaign brief. Structure per prompt:
- **Scene** — environment and staging
- **Subject** — character description (generic enough for Higgsfield casting, specific enough for brand fit)
- **Action** — what happens on screen
- **Camera** — movement and shot type
- **Lighting** — mood and quality
- **Tone** — editorial direction for Higgsfield's style selector
- **On-screen text** — add this as a text overlay in post, not in the prompt itself

Keep each clip **8–15 seconds** for TikTok/Reels. Longer hooks lose 60%+ of viewers by second 3.

## Production Notes

**Characters:**  
- Age 22–35, visibly athletic but not bodybuilder-aesthetic — gym-goer, not physique competitor  
- Real body types that reflect the 22–40 active adult target  
- Avoid: fake smiles, posed gym shots, overly polished influencer looks

**Products in frame:**  
- Always show the actual product (jar/label visible where possible)  
- Never let product feel prop-styled — it should feel like it belongs in the scene naturally

**Color grading:**  
- Concepts 1–3 (Upgrade, Clean vs. Crash, Ingredient Truth): Cool-neutral, clinical, premium  
- Concept 4 (Real Athletes): Warm, true-to-life, slightly desaturated — not filtered  
- Concept 5 (Skeptic to Convert): Warm throughout — emotional arc benefits from warmth

**Sound (add in post):**  
- Concepts 1–3: Minimal ambient, no music or subtle low-energy electronic bed  
- Concepts 4–5: Trending gym/workout audio from TikTok sound library for organic feel

**Safe zones:**  
- TikTok 9:16: Keep subject and product out of top 15% (profile/menu) and bottom 20% (CTA bar)  
- Meta Reels 9:16: Keep key action in center 80% of frame
