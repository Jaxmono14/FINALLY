## Brand DNA Summary
Proven. speaks with a direct, science-forward, bold voice (bold 8/10, innovative 7/10, rational-leaning 3/10) that refuses to sacrifice taste for clean ingredients — positioning itself as the rare brand that owns both. Visually, the brand lives in minimal, high-contrast professional photography with generous negative space, hero product angles, and athletes in authentic motion; the mood is high-performance, premium, scientific, and confident. The target is active adults 22–40 — gym-goers and competitive amateur athletes — fed up with chalky powders, untrustworthy labels, jittery pre-workouts, and supplements that don't fit their on-the-go lifestyle.

## Campaign Concepts

### Concept 1: The Gummy Upgrade
**Hypothesis:** The entire premium performance category is powder-only — positioning gummies as a direct "upgrade" from the chalky status quo converts powder-fatigued lifters who already accept they need supplements but hate the ritual.
**Primary Message:** Stop choking down chalky powder. Same science, now in a gummy.
**Tone:** Bold 8/10, innovative 7/10, rational 3/10 — direct and a little provocative, leading with format superiority.
**Copy Framework:** BAB (Before, After, Bridge)
**Visual Direction A (Photography):** Split composition — left side: matte-black powder tub with a clouded, dusty scoop mid-pour, slightly desaturated; right side: Proven. gummy jar with clean gummies caught mid-drop against a crisp white backdrop. High-contrast studio light, generous negative space down the vertical seam. Banana domain mode: Product.
**Visual Direction B (Illustration):** Minimal diagrammatic split — left: flat illustration of a chalky cloud with a "before" silhouette of a powder scoop in muted greys; right: precise geometric rendering of a gummy with a clean glow arc behind it. Abstract, editorial-grade. Banana domain mode: Abstract.
**Target Platforms:** Meta (high-intent comparison format), TikTok (the format-switch reveal hooks in first 2 seconds), Google PMax (visual asset).
**CTA:** Shop Gummies
**Addresses:** General brand awareness + category differentiation (format ownership vs. powder-only competitors).

### Concept 2: Clean vs. Crash
**Hypothesis:** Pre-workout buyers have been conditioned to expect jitters, tingles, and post-session crashes — reframing clean energy as the real performance advantage flips a category negative into Proven.'s wedge.
**Primary Message:** Energy that builds your workout — not one that sabotages it.
**Tone:** Bold 8/10, serious 5/10, emotional-leaning 4/10 — confident, no hype-speak, grounded in control rather than chaos.
**Copy Framework:** PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solution)
**Visual Direction A (Photography):** Athlete mid-set — controlled compound lift or deep sprint stride — captured in crisp clean studio light with soft rim lighting. No sweat-drama or shaky handheld. Composure on the face, explosive body. Cool neutral backdrop, generous margin. Banana domain mode: Portrait (Editorial).
**Visual Direction B (Illustration):** Split dual-line visualization — one line jagged with sharp peaks and a crash drop, the other a steady rising curve — layered behind an editorial silhouette of an athlete. Minimal, data-driven, restrained color. Banana domain mode: Abstract.
**Target Platforms:** Meta (problem-framed video performs well in feed), TikTok (PAS hook lands in first 3 seconds), Google (search intent around "clean pre workout").
**CTA:** Shop Pre-Workout
**Addresses:** Pain point — pre-workout products with crashes or jitters (from brand profile).

### Concept 3: Ingredient Truth
**Hypothesis:** Transparent Labs and Momentous have conditioned this audience to scrutinize labels — leaning into radical ingredient clarity meets them where they already are, while reinforcing Proven.'s science-backed brand value.
**Primary Message:** Every ingredient earns its place. Read the label — we dare you.
**Tone:** Formal 6/10, rational 3/10, expert 6/10 — clinical confidence, no marketing fluff, label-forward.
**Copy Framework:** FAB (Feature, Advantage, Benefit)
**Visual Direction A (Photography):** Overhead flat lay, clinical-clean. Gummy jar and powder tub centered, label facing camera and legible, surrounded by a minimal arrangement of whole-ingredient cues (not literal vegetables — more abstract clinical-pharmaceutical styling). Pure white sweep background, soft even light, zero shadow drama. Banana domain mode: Product.
**Visual Direction B (Illustration):** Exploded diagram — product at center with clean vector lines radiating outward to abstract molecular/ingredient glyphs. Technical-manual aesthetic, monochrome with one accent color pulse. Generous white space. Banana domain mode: UI/Web.
**Target Platforms:** Meta (carousel for ingredient-by-ingredient reveal), Google (supports branded + ingredient-research search queries), TikTok (label-flip reveal format).
**CTA:** See What's Inside
**Addresses:** Pain point — ingredient labels they can't trust or understand (from brand profile); reinforces brand value of transparency.

### Concept 4: Real Athletes, Real Results
**Hypothesis:** In a category saturated with polished studio ads, raw UGC-style creative signals authenticity and drives meaningfully higher CTR on Meta and TikTok — especially when the "taste reaction" beat is captured genuinely.
**Primary Message:** The only supplement my gym bag actually wants to be seen with.
**Tone:** Casual 6/10, playful 5/10, accessible 7/10 — peer-to-peer, zero corporate polish, real voice.
**Copy Framework:** Star-Story-Solution
**Visual Direction A (Photography):** Post-workout moment in an authentic gym environment — chalk dust, real sweat, imperfect light. Athlete taking a Proven. gummy, mid-taste-reaction (genuine, not performed). Slightly handheld framing, shallow depth of field, warm true-to-life color. Banana domain mode: Portrait (Editorial).
**Visual Direction B (Illustration):** Phone-screen-style composition mimicking a screen-recorded UGC clip — rough frame, timestamp, natural gym backdrop rendered in loose editorial illustration. Slight grain texture. Banana domain mode: Abstract.
**Target Platforms:** TikTok (native UGC performs 3–5x studio creative), Meta Reels (UGC halo effect), Meta Feed (as social proof variant).
**CTA:** Try It — 30-Day Guarantee
**Addresses:** Pain point — supplements that taste awful (from brand profile); social proof gap in DTC supplement category.
