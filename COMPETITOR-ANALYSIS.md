### Weaknesses & Vulnerabilities

This is where Proven. can attack.

#### 1. Product Quality — Creatine Stability in Gummy Form 🔴 CRITICAL
Multiple third-party tests found that Create's creatine gummies **failed label claim testing**:
- Creatine converts to creatinine (a waste metabolite) during the gummy manufacturing process (water-based process degrades creatine)
- Gummies appeared "shiny and clear" rather than "cloudy and grainy" (sign of proper undissolved creatine)
- Industry labs noted difficulty in accurately testing creatine gummies due to lack of standardized gummy testing protocols
- Customer complaints about gummies being "too hard" and having an off taste

**Proven. opportunity:** If Proven.'s gummies use a superior delivery format, manufacturing process, or creatine form (e.g., buffered creatine, creatine HCl, or a proprietary encapsulation), this is a meaningful differentiator. "Actually dosed right" vs. "tastes good but underdelivers" is a powerful attack angle.

#### 2. Dosing Math Problem 🟠 HIGH
- 1.5g creatine per gummy × 3 gummies = 4.5g dose (below the 5g clinical standard)
- 45 gummies ÷ 3 gummies/serving = 15 servings per container
- At $24.99, that's **$1.67/serving** — expensive for creatine monohydrate
- Powder creatine: 5g/serving at ~$0.20–0.40/serving
- Create is 4–8× more expensive per gram of creatine than powder

**Proven. opportunity:** If Proven.'s pre-workout or performance gummies deliver clinical doses at a more efficient price-per-dose, make that comparison explicit in ad creative.

#### 3. Narrow Product Range 🟠 HIGH
Create sells **creatine only** (now adding electrolytes). Their entire brand is built on one ingredient.

**Proven. opportunity:** Proven. offers a full performance suite — pre-workout, recovery, daily performance. For an athlete who needs multiple supplements, Proven. is a one-stop shop. "We cover your whole routine, not just one ingredient" is a strong angle.

#### 4. Wellness-Only Positioning Leaves Performance Athletes Underserved 🟡 MEDIUM
Create's "creatine for everybody" wellness framing intentionally de-emphasizes serious athletic performance. This appeals to casual users but potentially alienates competitive gym-goers who want performance-first messaging.

**Proven. opportunity:** Own the performance athlete. "Built for athletes who train seriously, not just anyone trying to be healthier." Proven.'s science-backed, results-driven voice speaks to the gym-goer that Create's inclusive wellness tone doesn't fully serve.

#### 5. Price Stability Issues 🟡 MEDIUM
Customer complaints about **4× price increases in less than 3 months** post-retail launch. Trust damage when pricing feels opportunistic.

**Proven. opportunity:** Lead with price consistency and value-based pricing. "Transparent pricing. Always." aligns with the clean/transparency brand values.

#### 6. No Pre-Workout, No Recovery, No Full Stack 🟡 MEDIUM
Create is creatine + electrolytes. It cannot serve a customer looking for pre-workout energy, recovery, or a full supplement stack.

### How Proven. Should Compete Against Create

#### Strategy 1: Own "Performance Gummies" — Create Owns "Wellness Gummies"
Create has carved out wellness. Proven. should carve out **performance**. These are different audiences and different purchase triggers:
- Create buyer: "I want to be healthier and creatine seems interesting"
- Proven. buyer: "I train hard and I need supplements that actually perform"

Don't try to out-wellness Create. Out-perform them.

#### Strategy 2: Attack Clinical Dosing
Run ad creative (especially on Google Search and TikTok) around **proper dosing**:
- "5g creatine per serving — the clinical standard. Not 4.5g."
- "Clinically dosed. Actually works."
- "Check the label — we'll wait."

This directly addresses Create's documented weakness without naming them.

#### Strategy 3: Full-Stack vs. Single-Ingredient
Create is one-dimensional. Proven. is a complete performance ecosystem:
- "Your creatine gummy covers one supplement. Proven. covers your whole training stack."
- "Pre-workout. Recovery. Performance. In a gummy. That's Proven."

#### Strategy 4: UGC from Performance Athletes, Not Wellness Influencers
Create's UGC is wellness/lifestyle influencers. Proven.'s UGC should be:
- Competitive CrossFitters
- Amateur powerlifters
- Runners hitting PRs
- Weekend warriors with real measurable results

The tone and authenticity signal to a performance-oriented buyer is completely different from what Create is producing.

#### Strategy 5: Taste + Performance (Create Only Claims Taste)
Create's main differentiator is convenience and taste. Proven. can own **taste AND performance**:
- "Clean. Proven. Performance. And yes — it actually tastes amazing."
- Create's tagline is essentially "creatine made easy." Proven.'s should be "performance made delicious."
